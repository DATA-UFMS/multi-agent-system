The field of digital marketing is ever-expanding, so it’s time to take a look at some of the most important digital marketing statistics and trends that will impact your business. 

While some concepts, like creating useful, trustworthy content for your audience, haven’t gone anywhere, there are a few things to look out for as you plan for the future of your business. 

In this article, we’ll give you the latest on content marketing, social media, SEO, and marketing automation to make your campaigns run more efficiently. 

##  Digital marketing benefits and ROI statistics 

Digital marketing offers new opportunities for your business. Since it’s highly measurable and adaptable, you can customize your digital marketing efforts to meet your business goals. 

  1. 86% of marketers increased brand awareness by using one or more digital marketing channels. ( [ Content Marketing Institute ](https://contentmarketinginstitute.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/10/2020_B2B_Research_Final.pdf) , 2020) 


  1. 79% of marketers educated their audience via digital marketing. (Content Marketing Institute, 2020) 


  1. 75% of marketers increased their company’s credibility and trust with digital marketing tactics. (Content Marketing Institute, 2020)

##  Content marketing stats 

Content marketing supports other digital marketing strategies such as SEO and email marketing. By creating high-quality, informative content, you’ll build a strong foundation for education and inspiring your audience that pays off big time. 

  1. 59% of B2C marketers increased their content marketing budgets in 2020 compared to 2019. ( [ Statista ](https://www.statista.com/statistics/331866/content-marketing-budget-change-north-america/) , 2020) 
  2. 83% of B2C content marketers use blog posts or short articles in their strategy. ( [ Statista ](https://www.statista.com/statistics/1115701/content-marketing-tactics-used-in-b2c-communications-in-north-america/) , 2020) 
  3. 74% of B2C marketers said they used email newsletters for content marketing. (Statista, 2020) 
  4. 38% of B2B company blogs publish content to educate their audience. ( [ Backlinko ](https://backlinko.com/b2b-content-marketing-report) , 2021) 
  5. 86% of B2B content marketers believe their audience sees them as trusted sources in their niche. ( [ Content Marketing Institute ](https://contentmarketinginstitute.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/10/2020_B2B_Research_Final.pdf) , 2020) 
  6. 85% of B2B content marketers use email marketing software to assist with content marketing. (Content Marketing Institute, 2020) 
  7. 88% of the most successful content marketers prioritize audience information over sales messages, compared to 50% of the least successful content marketers. (Content Marketing Institute, 2020)

##  Video marketing stats 

Video is a rapidly growing segment of content marketing. A video marketing strategy is more resource-intensive than developing a blog or email newsletter, but it comes with its own unique benefits and approaches. 

  1. Video marketing use has increased by 41% since 2016. ( [ Wyzowl ](https://www.wyzowl.com/video-marketing-statistics/) , 2020) 


  1. 87% of video marketers used YouTube last year, making it the most widely used video marketing platform for the seventh year in a row. (Wyzowl, 2020) 


  1. 96% of consumers have watched a video about a product or service. (Wyzowl, 2020) 


  1. 84% of consumers purchased a product because they watched a marketing video. (Wyzowl, 2020) 


  1. 78% of video marketers say video has directly helped increase sales (Wyzowl, 2020) 



Video marketing is a powerful tool for your digital marketing strategy, helping you reach new segments of your target audience. 

##  SEO statistics 

SEO, or search engine optimization, encompasses content creation, authority and link building, and technical elements to help your company website rank on Google and other search engines. It’s a complex digital marketing channel, so use these organic search statistics to keep track of current trends: 

  1. Small businesses spent $26.87 billion on SEO in 2020. ( [ Statista ](https://www.statista.com/statistics/665512/smb-digital-marketing-services-spend-usa/) , 2020) 


  1. Google makes up 87.7% of the global search engine share, followed by Bing at 5.56%. ( [ Statista ](https://www.statista.com/statistics/216573/worldwide-market-share-of-search-engines/) , 2021)

Global search engine market share 

  1. B2B blogs with educational content get 52% more organic traffic than B2B blogs with company-focused content. ( [ Backlinko ](https://backlinko.com/b2b-content-marketing-report) , 2021) 


  1. If you’re looking to build backlinks, longform content gets 77.2% more links than short articles. ( [ Backlinko ](https://backlinko.com/content-study) , 2019) 


  1. 40% of online search queries contain two keywords. ( [ Statista ](https://www.statista.com/statistics/269740/number-of-search-terms-in-internet-research-in-the-us/) , 2020) 


  1. 14.1% of all Google searches are in the form of a question. ( [ Backlinko ](https://backlinko.com/google-keyword-study) , 2020) 


  1. Only 2.4% of all Google search results have no SERP features, like People Also Ask questions, an image carousel, or a knowledge panel. (Backlinko, 2020) 



SEO works best when you inform your audience, not sell to them. By understanding keywords and search terms, you can tailor your content to both your industry and audience for maximum impact.

  1. 64% of social media marketers intend to increase brand awareness. (Hootsuite, 2021) 


  1. 45% of social media marketers are looking to drive conversions. (Hootsuite, 2021)

##  Email marketing stats
