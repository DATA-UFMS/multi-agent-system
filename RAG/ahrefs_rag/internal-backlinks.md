#  Internal backlinks 

The Internal backlinks report shows you the internal links on the target domain, URL, or subsection. 

![internal-backlinks-1](https://frontend-eu.s3.eu-central-1.amazonaws.com/internal-backlinks-1_7d358d6b8d595622482a62bedafa9b35.png)

Here are some actionable use cases for this report. 

##  Find nofollow internal links 

Nofollow internal links block Google from crawling content on your site and prevent the transfer of link equity. 

To find them, just add a  Nofollow  filter. Then analyze the report and remove the nofollow attribute on the link where relevant. 

![internal-backlinks-2](https://frontend-eu.s3.eu-central-1.amazonaws.com/internal-backlinks-2_c118acb40d821f64fbb265e3970d4fbe.png)

##  Find broken or redirected pages 

Find broken or redirected pages without having to run a crawl. Apply the  Target HTTP code  filter and either select 404 Not found (for broken pages) or 3XX redirect (for redirects). Then clean them up. 

![internal-backlinks-3](https://frontend-eu.s3.eu-central-1.amazonaws.com/internal-backlinks-3_5966664d7fb356fefda9ea15290addf5.png)

##  See if top-ranking pages are ranking due to internal links 

If you see a page ranking well in the organic search results with  [ few or no referring domains ](https://app.ahrefs.com/keywords-explorer/google/us/overview?keyword=best%20manga) , it might be thanks to strong internal links. 

![internal-backlinks-4](https://frontend-eu.s3.eu-central-1.amazonaws.com/internal-backlinks-4_a1ba02cca4bb9780ba7ecb7fb29e7728.png)

Here, it looks like japan-geeks.com has linked to their list of "best manga" from a number of internal pages that all have backlinks. 

![internal-backlinks-5](https://frontend-eu.s3.eu-central-1.amazonaws.com/internal-backlinks-5_f78860241a3f695f6b403b1fa403afb9.png)
