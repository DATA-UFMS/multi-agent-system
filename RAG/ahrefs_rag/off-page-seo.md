#  Off-page SEO 

Off-page SEO is anything you do outside your website to try to improve its search engine rankings. It‚Äôs often used synonymously with [ link building ](https://ahrefs.com/blog/link-building/) , but it goes beyond that. 

##  Why is it important? 

Just as your home‚Äôs value isn‚Äôt based solely on bricks and mortar but also on outside factors, like the neighborhood it‚Äôs in, the same is true of your website. Google looks at off-page factors like [ backlinks ](https://ahrefs.com/blog/what-are-backlinks/) and brand mentions to decide what you should rank for and where. [ [1]  ](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1eSg3ZFRm2sCb8OVIy9mHw8DGXFB74UE0mhOH6ewUFqQ/edit#references)

##  How does off-page differ from on-page SEO? 

From an SEO standpoint, the most important difference comes down to control. You have full control over on-page SEO but not off-page SEO. You can make all the changes or optimizations you like to your pages, but you can only ever influence (not fully control) off-page factors. 

![Control is the main difference between off-page and on-page SEO](https://ahrefs.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/off-page-seo-vs-on-page-seo.png)

Both are important, as confirmed by the respondents to [ my Twitter poll ](https://twitter.com/JoshuaCHardwick/status/1592548519222849537) .

##  Best practices 

Let‚Äôs look at a few examples of off-page SEO factors you can use to improve your rankings. 

###  Get more backlinks 

Backlinks are clickable links from one website to another, and they‚Äôre one of Google‚Äôs top ranking factors. [ [2]  ](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1eSg3ZFRm2sCb8OVIy9mHw8DGXFB74UE0mhOH6ewUFqQ/edit#references)

![Backlinks are clickable links from one website to another](https://ahrefs.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/1-backlinks.png)

However, not all links are created equal. Some will move the needle a lot, some a little, and some not at all. Links can even hurt rankings if Google sees them as link spam. [ [3]  ](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1eSg3ZFRm2sCb8OVIy9mHw8DGXFB74UE0mhOH6ewUFqQ/edit#references)

If you‚Äôre new to link building, we recommend starting with these three tactics: 

  * [ **Competitor link building** ](https://ahrefs.com/blog/find-competitors-backlinks/) **.** Find and replicate your competitors‚Äô best links. 
  * [ **Guest posting** ](https://ahrefs.com/blog/guest-blogging/) **.** Write a post for another site in return for a link. 
  * [ **Resource page link building** ](https://ahrefs.com/blog/resource-page-link-building/) **.** Pitch your content to pages that curate industry resources. 



For example, say you run an email marketing platform. You might start by entering Mailchimp‚Äôs homepage in a competitive analysis tool like Ahrefs‚Äô [ Site Explorer ](https://ahrefs.com/site-explorer) and looking for replicable links in the **Backlinks** report. 

![Backlinks report in Ahrefs' Site Explorer](https://ahrefs.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/2-backlinks-report.png)

Just make sure to go after links that are both easy to replicate and high-quality. 

![How to find the best links to replicate](https://ahrefs.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/best-links-to-replicate.png)

Here are three crucial things to check before pursuing a link: 

  * **Authority.** Is the linking site trustworthy? 
  * **Relevance.** Is the topic of the linking site and page similar to your content? 
  * **Placement.** Is the link likely to be in a prominent position on the page?

###  Get a Google Business Profile 

If you run a local business, like a convenience store or dental clinic, you need a Google Business Profile. This is because you can only show up for ‚Äúmap pack‚Äù results if you have one. 

![Map pack results in Google](https://ahrefs.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/3-map-pack.png)

Luckily, it‚Äôs completely free to create a Business Profile. Just head to [ google.com/business ](https://www.google.com/business/) , sign up, and fill in your details. 

Here‚Äôs our advice for optimizing your profile: 

  * Be as specific as you can with your business category 
  * Add your business hours (inc. holiday hours) 
  * Add your address (if you have a storefront) 
  * Set your service area (if you visit or deliver to customers and clients) 
  * Add your products/services 
  * Add photos 



**Learn more:** [ _How to Optimize Your Google Business Listing_ ](https://ahrefs.com/blog/google-my-business/)

###  Ask for reviews 

Reviews are a ranking factor for ‚Äúmap pack‚Äù results, and 17% of SEOs think they‚Äôre the most important factor. [ [4]  ](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1eSg3ZFRm2sCb8OVIy9mHw8DGXFB74UE0mhOH6ewUFqQ/edit#references)

![Many SEOs think reviews are a ranking factor](https://ahrefs.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/4-reviews-ranking-factor.png)

Their importance seems to be increasing over time, too. 

![The perceived importance of reviews as a ranking factor over time](https://ahrefs.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/5-reviews-ranking-factor-over-time.png)

It‚Äôs also likely that reviews across the web bear some importance for regular rankings. This is because they‚Äôre one of the things Google tells [ Search Quality Raters ](https://ahrefs.com/blog/google-quality-raters-guidelines/) to look for when evaluating a website‚Äôs Expertise, Authority, and Trustworthiness (E-A-T). 

> Look for reviews, references, recommendations by experts, news articles, and other credible information created/written by individuals about the website. 

SIDENOTE. E-A-T is not a direct ranking factor. Learn more about what this means in [ our E-A-T guide ](https://ahrefs.com/blog/eat-seo/) . 

Here‚Äôs our advice for getting more reviews: 

  * Ask customers to leave them 
  * Prioritize getting reviews on your Google Business Profile 
  * Build trust by responding to reviews 
  * Don‚Äôt offer or accept money in exchange for reviews (it‚Äôs against [ Google‚Äôs terms ](https://support.google.com/contributionpolicy/answer/7411351?hl=en&ref_topic=7422769) ) 
  * Don‚Äôt discourage bad reviews or request good reviews (it‚Äôs also against Google‚Äôs terms) 



###  Hire experts 

Given that Google is looking for content that demonstrates expertise, hiring expert writers will likely increase your chances of ranking. 

Here are a few reasons for this: 

  * People are more likely to trust and link to content written by experts. 
  * Google doesn‚Äôt use authorship in their algorithms anymore, but they do have ways of identifying authors. [ [5]  ](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1eSg3ZFRm2sCb8OVIy9mHw8DGXFB74UE0mhOH6ewUFqQ/edit#references) So they may rank content by experts higher. 



In fact, a recent uptick in our blog rankings coincided with one of our authors, Patrick Stox, winning a [ knowledge panel ](https://ahrefs.com/seo/glossary/google-knowledge-panel) . While causation doesn‚Äôt always equal causation, our gut feeling is that these two things were related. 

Just make sure to showcase the experts you hire as best you can. 

Here‚Äôs an excellent example of how to do this from Healthline: 

![Example of expertise being showcased from Healthline](https://ahrefs.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/6-healthline-expertise.png)

If you‚Äôre not sure where to start when it comes to hiring experts, read [ our guide to hiring freelance writers ](https://ahrefs.com/blog/hiring-freelance-writers/) .

Google‚Äôs Gary Illyes is on record saying that mentions help rankings.

Here are a few ways to get more brand mentions: 

  * Get featured on podcasts 
  * Get interviewed 
  * Respond to journalist requests via HARO 



For example, here‚Äôs a brand mention of Ahrefs thanks to a podcast interview with our Head of Content, Joshua Hardwick:

However, while brand mentions may help SEO, making efforts solely to try to get more of them likely isn‚Äôt the best use of your time. It makes more sense to do things with other marketing value (such as podcast interviews) and view the brand mentions as a nice bonus. 

If you want to get even more juice out of your brand mentions, consider an unlinked mention link building campaign. This is where you reach out to websites that have already mentioned you and ask them to add a link to your website.

##  FAQs 

###  Are social signals a ranking factor? 

No. Google‚Äôs Gary Illyes confirmed this in a 2016 interview. [ [5]  ](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1eSg3ZFRm2sCb8OVIy9mHw8DGXFB74UE0mhOH6ewUFqQ/edit#references)

###  Are brand searches a ranking factor? 

Even though branded searches often correlate with organic search traffic, there‚Äôs no evidence to suggest that they‚Äôre a direct ranking factor.
